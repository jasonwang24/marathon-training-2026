❌ Do NOT Roll These Areas
🦴Never roll directly over the lateral knee bone. This compresses the bursa between the band and bone, causing inflammation — exactly what you're trying to fix.
🚫Do not aggressively roll the IT band strap along the outer thigh. It's a tendon, not a muscle — it cannot release via compression. The pain you feel is nerve irritation, not a therapeutic release.
✅ DO Roll These Upstream Sources
🎯TFL (Tensor Fasciae Latae) — your hip pocket muscle. Find the front-top of your hip crest (ASIS), move 2 inches forward and down. Lie face-down/angled, roll slowly. Hold tender spots 20–30 sec. This is the primary driver of IT band tension.
🍑Gluteus Maximus & Medius — sit on the roller, cross one ankle over the opposite knee (figure-4 / pigeon). Roll the entire posterior hip. Weak glutes force the TFL to overwork, which is the root of most ITBS.
💎Piriformis (deep glute) — same pigeon position, focus on the area near your sit bone. 60–90 seconds each side.
Pro Protocol: Roll TFL → Glutes → Piriformis (3–5 min total). Finish with 10 slow hip circles each side. Do this after every run and before bed during recovery weeks.
